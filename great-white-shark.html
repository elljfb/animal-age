About the Great White Shark

The Great White Shark is a member of the Lamnidae family. It is primarily found in Worldwide and inhabits Oceans. This animal has a lifespan of 70 years and is known for its Gray, White color. Its diet consists of Carnivore, and it faces threats from Orcas. The Great White Shark has an average speed of 56 km/h and can reach a top speed of 56 km/h.

The Great White Shark has a Solitary social structure and gives birth to 2-17 offspring after a gestation period of 12-18 days. Its conservation status is currently Vulnerable.
