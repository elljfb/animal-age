About the Wild Boar

The Wild Boar is a member of the Suidae family. It is primarily found in Eurasia, North Africa, Americas and inhabits Forests, Grasslands. This animal has a lifespan of 10-25 years and is known for its Brown, Black color. Its diet consists of Omnivore, and it faces threats from Tigers, Leopards. The Wild Boar has an average speed of 20 km/h and can reach a top speed of 20 km/h.

The Wild Boar has a Group-based social structure and gives birth to 6-30 offspring after a gestation period of 160-190 days. Its conservation status is currently Least Concern.
