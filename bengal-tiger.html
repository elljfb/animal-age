About the Bengal Tiger

The Bengal Tiger is a member of the Felidae family. It is primarily found in India, Bangladesh, Nepal and inhabits Grasslands, Mangroves. This animal has a lifespan of 10-15 years and is known for its Orange, Black color. Its diet consists of Carnivore, and it faces threats from Humans, Crocodiles. The Bengal Tiger has an average speed of 65 km/h and can reach a top speed of 65 km/h.

The Bengal Tiger has a Solitary social structure and gives birth to 2-4 (usually) offspring after a gestation period of 104-106 days. Its conservation status is currently Endangered.
