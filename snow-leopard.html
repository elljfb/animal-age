About the Snow Leopard

The Snow Leopard is a member of the Felidae family. It is primarily found in Central and South Asia and inhabits Mountains, Steppes. This animal has a lifespan of 10-15 years and is known for its Grayish-yellow, Black color. Its diet consists of Carnivore, and it faces threats from Humans, Wolves. The Snow Leopard has an average speed of 40-60 km/h and can reach a top speed of 40-60 km/h.

The Snow Leopard has a Solitary social structure and gives birth to 1-5 offspring after a gestation period of 90-100 days. Its conservation status is currently Vulnerable.
