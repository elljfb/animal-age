About the Kangaroo Rat

The Kangaroo Rat is a member of the Heteromyidae family. It is primarily found in North America and inhabits Deserts. This animal has a lifespan of 2-5 years and is known for its Brown, Gray color. Its diet consists of Herbivore, and it faces threats from Snakes, Birds of Prey. The Kangaroo Rat has an average speed of 16 km/h and can reach a top speed of 16 km/h.

The Kangaroo Rat has a Solitary social structure and gives birth to 2-5 offspring after a gestation period of 22-24 days. Its conservation status is currently Least Concern.
