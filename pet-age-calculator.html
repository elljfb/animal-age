How Pet Age Calculation Works

The conversion from pet years to human years varies significantly between species and even breeds. Our calculator uses scientifically-based formulas:

  • Small Dogs: Age calculation uses a logarithmic formula to account for their unique aging pattern.
  • Medium Dogs: First two years count as 15 and 10 years respectively, then 5 years per year after.
  • Large Dogs: Similar to medium dogs but age faster, with 12 years for the second year and 7 years per year after.
  • Cats: 15 years for first year, 9 for second, then 4 years per year.
  • Rabbits: 20 years for first year, then varying rates after.
  • Hamsters: Age very quickly in their first months.
  • Birds: Age more slowly, with 10 years for the first year.
  • Reptiles: Generally age very slowly compared to mammals.

Tips for Pet Age Care

  • Regular Check-ups: As pets age, they need more frequent veterinary visits.
  • Diet Adjustments: Older pets may need special diets to maintain their health.
  • Exercise: Maintain regular exercise but adjust intensity for senior pets.
  • Mental Stimulation: Keep your pet's mind active with toys and games.
  • Breed-Specific Care: Different breeds may have different aging concerns.
