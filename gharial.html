About the Gharial

The Gharial is a member of the Gavialidae family. It is primarily found in Indian subcontinent and inhabits Rivers, Lakes. This animal has a lifespan of 40-60 years and is known for its Brownish-gray color. Its diet consists of Carnivore, and it faces threats from Crocodiles, Tigers. The Gharial has an average speed of 32 km/h and can reach a top speed of 32 km/h.

The Gharial has a Solitary social structure and gives birth to 20-95 offspring after a gestation period of 94-123 days. Its conservation status is currently Critically Endangered.
