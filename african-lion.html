About the African Lion

The African Lion is a member of the Felidae family. It is primarily found in Africa and inhabits Grasslands, Savannas. This animal has a lifespan of 10-14 years and is known for its Tan color. Its diet consists of Carnivore, and it faces threats from Hyenas, Crocodiles. The African Lion has an average speed of 58 km/h and can reach a top speed of 80 km/h.

The African Lion has a Group-based social structure and gives birth to 2-4 (usually) offspring after a gestation period of 98-105 days. Its conservation status is currently Vulnerable.
