About the Western Gorilla

The Western Gorilla is a member of the Hominidae family. It is primarily found in Central Africa and inhabits Rainforests. This animal has a lifespan of 30-40 years and is known for its Black color. Its diet consists of Herbivore, and it faces threats from Leopards, Humans. The Western Gorilla has an average speed of 40 km/h and can reach a top speed of 40 km/h.

The Western Gorilla has a Social groups social structure and gives birth to 1 offspring after a gestation period of 220-270 days. Its conservation status is currently Critically Endangered.
