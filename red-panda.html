About the Red Panda

The Red Panda is a member of the Ailuridae family. It is primarily found in China, Nepal, Bhutan, India and inhabits Himalayan Foothills. This animal has a lifespan of 8-12 years and is known for its Reddish-brown color. Its diet consists of Herbivore, and it faces threats from Snow Leopards, Martens. The Red Panda has an average speed of 0.2-0.4 km/h and can reach a top speed of 0.2-0.4 km/h.

The Red Panda has a Solitary social structure and gives birth to 1-4 offspring after a gestation period of 112-158 days. Its conservation status is currently Endangered.
