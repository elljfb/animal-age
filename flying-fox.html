About the Flying Fox

The Flying Fox is a member of the Pteropodidae family. It is primarily found in Asia, Australia and inhabits Forests. This animal has a lifespan of 15-23 years and is known for its Brown, Gray color. Its diet consists of Herbivore, and it faces threats from Birds of Prey. The Flying Fox has an average speed of 40-50 km/h and can reach a top speed of 40-50 km/h.

The Flying Fox has a Group-based social structure and gives birth to 1 offspring after a gestation period of 140-150 days. Its conservation status is currently Least Concern.
