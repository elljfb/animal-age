About the Lion-tailed Macaque

The Lion-tailed Macaque is a member of the Cercopithecidae family. It is primarily found in India and inhabits Western Ghats, India. This animal has a lifespan of 20-30 years and is known for its Black color. Its diet consists of Omnivore, Herbivore, and it faces threats from Tigers, Leopards. The Lion-tailed Macaque has an average speed of 30-40 km/h and can reach a top speed of 35 km/h.

The Lion-tailed Macaque has a Social groups social structure and gives birth to 1 offspring after a gestation period of 150-170 days. Its conservation status is currently Endangered.
