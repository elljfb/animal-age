About the Burmese Python

The Burmese Python is a member of the Pythonidae family. It is primarily found in Southeast Asia, South Asia, Indonesia and inhabits Grasslands, Forests. This animal has a lifespan of 20-25 years and is known for its Brown, Black color. Its diet consists of Carnivore, and it faces threats from Leopards, Hawksbills. The Burmese Python has an average speed of 1.6-2.4 km/h and can reach a top speed of 1.6-2.4 km/h.

The Burmese Python has a Solitary social structure and gives birth to 30-80 offspring after a gestation period of 88-100 days. Its conservation status is currently Least Concern.
