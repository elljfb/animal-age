About the African Wild Dog

The African Wild Dog is a member of the Canidae family. It is primarily found in Sub-Saharan Africa and inhabits Savannahs. This animal has a lifespan of 10-12 years and is known for its Multicolored color. Its diet consists of Carnivore, and it faces threats from Lions, Hyenas. The African Wild Dog has an average speed of 56 km/h and can reach a top speed of 56 km/h.

The African Wild Dog has a Group-based social structure and gives birth to 10-12 offspring after a gestation period of 70 days. Its conservation status is currently Endangered.
