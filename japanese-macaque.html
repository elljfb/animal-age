About the Japanese Macaque

The Japanese Macaque is a member of the Cercopithecidae family. It is primarily found in Japan, China, North Korea and inhabits Forests, Mountains. This animal has a lifespan of 20-30 years and is known for its Brown color. Its diet consists of Omnivore, and it faces threats from Humans, Leopards. The Japanese Macaque has an average speed of 30 km/h and can reach a top speed of 30 km/h.

The Japanese Macaque has a Social groups social structure and gives birth to 1 offspring after a gestation period of 173-194 days. Its conservation status is currently Least Concern.
