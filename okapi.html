About the Okapi

The Okapi is a member of the Giraffidae family. It is primarily found in Democratic Republic of Congo and inhabits Rainforests. This animal has a lifespan of 20-30 years and is known for its Dark Brown, White color. Its diet consists of Herbivore, and it faces threats from Leopards, Humans. The Okapi has an average speed of 58 km/h and can reach a top speed of 58 km/h.

The Okapi has a Solitary social structure and gives birth to 1 offspring after a gestation period of 420-450 days. Its conservation status is currently Endangered.
