About the Galápagos Penguin

The Galápagos Penguin is a member of the Spheniscidae family. It is primarily found in Galápagos Islands and inhabits Galápagos Islands. This animal has a lifespan of 15-20 years and is known for its Black, White color. Its diet consists of Carnivore, Piscivore, and it faces threats from Sharks, Birds. The Galápagos Penguin has an average speed of 6-7 km/h and can reach a top speed of 35 km/h.

The Galápagos Penguin has a Colony-based social structure and gives birth to 1 offspring after a gestation period of 38-40 days. Its conservation status is currently Endangered.
