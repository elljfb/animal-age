About the Blue Jay

The Blue Jay is a member of the Corvidae family. It is primarily found in North America and inhabits Forests, Urban Areas. This animal has a lifespan of 6-15 years and is known for its Blue, White color. Its diet consists of Omnivore, and it faces threats from Birds of Prey, Snakes. The Blue Jay has an average speed of 32 km/h and can reach a top speed of 32 km/h.

The Blue Jay has a Flocks social structure and gives birth to 2-6 offspring after a gestation period of 17-18 days. Its conservation status is currently Least Concern.
