About the Platypus

The Platypus is a member of the Ornithorhynchidae family. It is primarily found in Australia and inhabits Eastern Australia. This animal has a lifespan of 10-17 years and is known for its Brown color. Its diet consists of Carnivore, Insectivore, and it faces threats from Foxes, Birds of Prey. The Platypus has an average speed of 3-7 km/h and can reach a top speed of 9 km/h.

The Platypus has a Solitary social structure and gives birth to 1-3 (rarely 5) offspring after a gestation period of 10 days (in burrow) days. Its conservation status is currently Near Threatened.
