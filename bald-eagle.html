About the Bald Eagle

The Bald Eagle is a member of the Accipitridae family. It is primarily found in North America and inhabits Forests, Lakes, Coasts. This animal has a lifespan of 20-30 years and is known for its Brown, White color. Its diet consists of Carnivore, and it faces threats from Wolves, Raccoons. The Bald Eagle has an average speed of 120 km/h and can reach a top speed of 120 km/h.

The Bald Eagle has a Solitary social structure and gives birth to 1-3 offspring after a gestation period of 34-36 days. Its conservation status is currently Least Concern.
