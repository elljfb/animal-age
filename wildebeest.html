About the Wildebeest

The Wildebeest is a member of the Bovidae family. It is primarily found in Eastern and Southern Africa and inhabits Grasslands. This animal has a lifespan of 20-25 years and is known for its Gray, Black color. Its diet consists of Herbivore, and it faces threats from Lions, Crocodiles. The Wildebeest has an average speed of 47 km/h and can reach a top speed of 47 km/h.

The Wildebeest has a Group-based social structure and gives birth to 1-1000 offspring after a gestation period of 240-270 days. Its conservation status is currently Least Concern.
