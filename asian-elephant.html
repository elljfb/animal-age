About the Asian Elephant

The Asian Elephant is a member of the Elephantidae family. It is primarily found in Asia (Southeast) and inhabits Grasslands, Forests. This animal has a lifespan of 60-80 years and is known for its Grey color. Its diet consists of Herbivore, and it faces threats from Tigers, Leopards. The Asian Elephant has an average speed of 40 km/h and can reach a top speed of 40 km/h.

The Asian Elephant has a Herd-based social structure and gives birth to 1 offspring after a gestation period of 640-660 days. Its conservation status is currently Endangered.
