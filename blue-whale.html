About the Blue Whale

The Blue Whale is a member of the Balaenopteridae family. It is primarily found in Oceans worldwide and inhabits Oceans. This animal has a lifespan of 70-90 years and is known for its Blue-grey color. Its diet consists of Filter Feeder, and it faces threats from Orcas. The Blue Whale has an average speed of 40 km/h and can reach a top speed of 39 km/h.

The Blue Whale has a Solitary social structure and gives birth to 1 (rarely 2) offspring after a gestation period of 10-12 months days. Its conservation status is currently Endangered.
