About the Chimpanzee

The Chimpanzee is a member of the Hominidae family. It is primarily found in Central and West Africa and inhabits Rainforests, Grasslands. This animal has a lifespan of 40-50 years and is known for its Black color. Its diet consists of Omnivore, and it faces threats from Leopards, Humans. The Chimpanzee has an average speed of 40 km/h and can reach a top speed of 40 km/h.

The Chimpanzee has a Social groups social structure and gives birth to 1 offspring after a gestation period of 230-240 days. Its conservation status is currently Endangered.
