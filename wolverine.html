About the Wolverine

The Wolverine is a member of the Mustelidae family. It is primarily found in North America, Europe, Asia and inhabits Boreal Forests, Tundra. This animal has a lifespan of 7-12 years and is known for its Dark Brown color. Its diet consists of Carnivore, Scavenger, and it faces threats from Bears, Wolves. The Wolverine has an average speed of 56-70 km/h and can reach a top speed of 56-70 km/h.

The Wolverine has a Solitary social structure and gives birth to 2-3 offspring after a gestation period of 40-45 days. Its conservation status is currently Least Concern.
