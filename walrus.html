About the Walrus

The Walrus is a member of the Odobenidae family. It is primarily found in Arctic, Subarctic and inhabits Arctic, Coastal Areas. This animal has a lifespan of 40-50 years and is known for its Brown color. Its diet consists of Carnivore, and it faces threats from Polar Bears, Orcas. The Walrus has an average speed of 40 km/h and can reach a top speed of 40 km/h.

The Walrus has a Group-based social structure and gives birth to Up to 200 offspring after a gestation period of 20-25 days. Its conservation status is currently Least Concern.
